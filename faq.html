FAQ

Frequently asked questions

If your question isn't answered here, reach out on the Contact page — we read every message.

What is TicketStub, exactly?

TicketStub is an Android app that converts photos into ticket-style memory cards, complete with a date, location, and personal note. It's designed for keeping personal memories — concerts, trips, meals, small everyday moments — in a format that's more meaningful to revisit than a plain camera roll.

Is TicketStub free to use?

Yes. TicketStub is free to download and use for creating and browsing your stub collection. Certain optional extras are offered as in-app purchases; the core memory-keeping features do not require payment.

Does TicketStub show ads?

No. The TicketStub app itself does not currently display third-party ads.

Do I need an account?

Yes — TicketStub uses a simple email-and-password account so your stub collection can sync across devices and survive an app reinstall. We don't require any social account linking, and you won't get a public profile or follower list; accounts exist purely to keep your personal collection safe and portable.

Where is my data stored?

Your account details and stubs (photos, notes, dates, locations) are stored securely using Supabase, our backend infrastructure provider. Full details are in our Privacy Policy.

Can I use TicketStub offline?

You can browse and create stubs while offline; changes sync to your account the next time you're connected.

How do I delete my account and data?

Email workwithme785@gmail.com from your registered address with the subject "TicketStub Account Deletion Request." Full details, including exactly what gets removed and how long it takes, are on our account deletion page.

Can I share my stubs with other people?

Not yet — TicketStub is currently a private, single-user archive. Sharing and export options are on the roadmap; see the Updates page for progress.

Who built TicketStub?

TicketStub is developed and maintained solo by Pranav Kadam, an independent Android developer based in Pune, India, under the studio name Daily Tools HQ. Read more on the About page.

How do I report a bug or request a feature?

Email us directly — see the Contact page for the address and what to include so we can help faster.

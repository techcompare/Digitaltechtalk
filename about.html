Updates

What's new in TicketStub

Development notes and release history — a running log of how the app is evolving.

July 2026

Companion website launched

TicketStub now has a proper home on the web — this site. Alongside the Play Store listing, it covers what the app does, how it handles your data, and answers to the questions we get most often. The privacy policy and account deletion pages have also been expanded with clearer detail on how Supabase is used to store and sync your stubs.

Q2 2026

Account sync via Supabase

Stub collections now sync reliably across devices using Supabase-backed accounts, so switching phones or reinstalling the app no longer means losing your archive. This replaced an earlier local-only storage approach that couldn't survive an uninstall.

Q1 2026

Search and collection browsing

Added the ability to search stubs by title, note, or location, along with a refreshed collection view designed to feel closer to flipping through a physical box of kept tickets than scrolling a photo grid.

Late 2025

First public release

TicketStub launched on Google Play with its core feature: turning a photo into a ticket-stub-style memory card with a date, location, and note attached.

What's coming next

Planned work includes richer stub templates, an export/backup option for your archive, and early exploration of optional collection sharing. There's no fixed timeline — TicketStub is built and maintained solo, and features ship when they're ready rather than on a set schedule. Check back here for progress, or get in touch on the Contact page with what you'd like to see next.
